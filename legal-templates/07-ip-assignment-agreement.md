# CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION AND INVENTION ASSIGNMENT AGREEMENT

**This Confidential Information and Invention Assignment Agreement** (this "Agreement") is entered into as of **[EFFECTIVE_DATE]** by and between:

- **Nesteye, Inc.**, a Delaware corporation ("Company"); and
- **[INDIVIDUAL_NAME]**, an individual residing at **[ADDRESS]** ("you").

This Agreement covers your work for the Company as a **[ROLE — Founder, Employee, Contractor, or Advisor]**, regardless of whether you are paid or unpaid, and applies to any work you performed for or related to the Company before the Effective Date (the "Prior Work").

## 1. Confidential Information

### 1.1 Definition
"Confidential Information" means any non-public information you receive, develop, or have access to in connection with your relationship with the Company, including:

- Business plans, financial information, customer and supplier lists, pricing, and marketing strategies.
- Source code, firmware, hardware designs, schematics, bills of materials, manufacturing processes, machine learning models, training data, model weights, and inference architectures.
- Research, development plans, product roadmaps, and unreleased features.
- Information about pilots, farm partners, and integrator discussions.
- Any imagery, video, sensor data, or derived metrics from poultry barns (collectively, "Farm Data").
- Any third-party confidential information disclosed to the Company under a non-disclosure obligation.

### 1.2 Obligations
You will hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence, use it only to perform your work for the Company, and not disclose it to any third party or use it for your own benefit or the benefit of any third party. You will protect Confidential Information with at least the same degree of care you use for your own confidential information of similar importance and in no event less than reasonable care.

### 1.3 Survival
Your confidentiality obligations continue indefinitely with respect to trade secrets and for **[PERIOD — recommend 5] years** after the end of your relationship with the Company for other Confidential Information.

### 1.4 Exclusions
Confidential Information does not include information that (a) is or becomes publicly known through no fault of yours; (b) you can demonstrate was in your lawful possession before the Company disclosed it to you, without an obligation of confidentiality; (c) you independently develop without use of or reference to the Company's Confidential Information; or (d) is rightfully obtained from a third party without an obligation of confidentiality.

### 1.5 Defend Trade Secrets Act Notice
Under the federal Defend Trade Secrets Act of 2016, you are notified that you may not be held criminally or civilly liable under any federal or state trade secret law for disclosure of a trade secret that is (a) made in confidence to a federal, state, or local government official or to an attorney, solely for the purpose of reporting or investigating a suspected violation of law; or (b) made in a complaint or other document filed in a lawsuit or other proceeding, if such filing is made under seal.

## 2. Inventions

### 2.1 Definitions
"Invention" means any idea, concept, design, work of authorship, software, model, dataset, hardware design, improvement, discovery, process, method, technique, formula, or composition of matter, whether or not patentable or copyrightable, that you make, conceive of, reduce to practice, develop, or author, alone or with others.

"Company Invention" means any Invention that:
- Relates to the Company's actual or anticipated business, research, or development (including poultry monitoring, edge AI, computer vision for livestock, weight estimation, environmental monitoring, behavior intervention, or related fields); or
- Was made or developed using the Company's equipment, supplies, facilities, Confidential Information, or time.

### 2.2 Prior Inventions
You have listed in **Exhibit A** all inventions or intellectual property you own or have an interest in as of the Effective Date that you wish to exclude from this Agreement ("Prior Inventions"). If you do not list any, you represent that you have none. Prior Inventions remain your property. However, if you incorporate any Prior Invention into a Company product or service, you grant the Company a non-exclusive, worldwide, royalty-free, perpetual, irrevocable, sublicensable license to make, use, modify, and distribute that Prior Invention as part of Company products and services.

### 2.3 Assignment of Company Inventions
You hereby irrevocably assign to the Company all your right, title, and interest in and to all Company Inventions, including any Prior Work that constitutes a Company Invention, throughout the world, in perpetuity. This assignment includes all intellectual property rights, including patents, copyrights, trade secrets, mask work rights, and moral rights, and the right to sue for past, present, and future infringement.

### 2.4 Works Made for Hire
You acknowledge that, to the extent permitted by law, all Company Inventions that qualify as works of authorship are "works made for hire" under the U.S. Copyright Act, and the Company is the owner thereof. To the extent any Company Invention is not a work made for hire, you assign it to the Company as set out in Section 2.3.

### 2.5 Moral Rights Waiver
To the extent you have any moral rights, rights of attribution, or rights of integrity in any Company Invention, you waive those rights to the maximum extent permitted by law.

### 2.6 Further Assurances
You will execute any documents, assist in the prosecution of any patent or other intellectual property application, and otherwise cooperate at the Company's reasonable request (and at the Company's expense) to perfect the Company's rights in Company Inventions, including after the end of your relationship with the Company.

### 2.7 Power of Attorney
If the Company is unable to secure your signature on any document needed to apply for or prosecute any intellectual property right in a Company Invention after reasonable effort, you appoint the Company and its duly authorized officers as your attorney-in-fact, with full power of substitution, to act on your behalf to execute and file such documents. This power is coupled with an interest and is irrevocable.

### 2.8 State Law Carve-Outs (where applicable)
This Agreement's assignment of Inventions does not apply to any Invention that fully qualifies under any applicable state law that limits assignment of inventions made entirely on the employee's own time without using employer equipment, supplies, facilities, or trade secret information. **[NOTE: Texas does not have a statutory carve-out like California's Labor Code § 2870, but this language is included as a belt-and-suspenders measure for any future employees in carve-out states.]**

## 3. Third-Party Information

You will not, in your work for the Company, use or disclose any confidential information or trade secrets of any former employer or any other third party, except with the prior written authorization of that third party. You will not bring onto the Company's premises any unpublished documents or property belonging to a former employer.

## 4. No Conflicts

You represent that you are not subject to any prior agreement that would prevent you from entering into this Agreement or assigning Company Inventions to the Company. You will not enter into any other agreement that conflicts with this Agreement.

## 5. Return of Materials

At the end of your relationship with the Company, or earlier if requested, you will promptly return all Company property and Confidential Information in your possession or control, including all copies and derivatives, and will not retain any copies, except with the Company's written consent.

## 6. Non-Solicitation (optional)

**[OPTIONAL — strike if Company prefers no non-solicit]** For a period of **[PERIOD — recommend 12 months]** after the end of your relationship with the Company, you will not, directly or indirectly, solicit any Company employee or contractor to terminate his or her relationship with the Company, or solicit any Company customer with respect to a competing product. This Section does not prohibit general advertising or hiring of persons who respond to such advertising.

## 7. No Employment Commitment

This Agreement does not create an employment relationship and does not entitle you to any compensation, equity, or continued engagement. Any such terms are addressed in a separate offer letter, employment agreement, equity grant, or services agreement.
